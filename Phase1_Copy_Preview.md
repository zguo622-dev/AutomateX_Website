## 7. NEW — "Beliefs blocking you" / Mistakes block

**This doesn't exist on the site today. We add it between the three insight cards and the PACED chart, as a new full-width section titled:**

> `The beliefs keeping owners stuck`

**Subhead:** `Six wrong assumptions we hear from founders every month. Each one is the reason a firm that's still winning today gets priced out in 18 months.`

**Six cards (from v3 canvas, titles bold, rebuttal below):**

1. **"AI is hype. We win on relationships."**
   True for the last 20 years. Not true for the next 5. The Rolodex wins clients; it doesn't protect margin when procurement runs a price comparison.

2. **"We need to build our own platform or ignore it."**
   False binary. The right move is targeted pilots on one workflow with measurable ROI — not a £200k "digital transformation" project that dies in year two.

3. **"I'll lose my best engineers if I automate."**
   Opposite is true. Engineers leave because they're doing Form G admin, not engineering. Automate the admin and they stay for the interesting work.

4. **"My technical director should run this."**
   They won't. They're billable. This is an owner's decision because only the owner feels the downside if it fails.

5. **"I'll wait and see who wins."**
   By the time there's a winner, the acquirer has already started rolling up the market. You're either the consolidator or you're sold.

6. **"I'll hire someone to handle it internally."**
   A £90k AI hire who's never delivered rail assurance wastes 18 months learning the domain. You need domain + tech in one person, which is why it's a consulting spend, not a hire.

**Why this is a big unlock.** Every owner-operator buying conversation gets stuck on one of these six. Putting them on the page, with a direct counter, lets the right buyer self-qualify and disarm their own objection before the call. It also signals "I understand how you actually think" — which is half the reason owners hire specialists at all.
